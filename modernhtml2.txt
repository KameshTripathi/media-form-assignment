HTML entities are a way to represent special characters in HTML. They are used to display characters that are not available on the keyboard, or to display characters that have special meaning in HTML.

Here are 5 commonly used HTML entities:

&amp; - This entity represents the ampersand (&) character.
&lt; - This entity represents the less-than (<) character.
&gt; - This entity represents the greater-than (>) character.
&quot; - This entity represents the double quotation mark (") character.
&apos; - This entity represents the single quotation mark (') character.
